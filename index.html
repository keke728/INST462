Course Description

This course explores how data becomes meaningful, interpretable, and expressive through visualization. Blending design, storytelling, accessibility, and interaction, students learn how to transform raw information into compelling visual and spatial experiences. Each week builds toward a deeper understanding of how people see, feel, and make sense of data‚Äîacross screens, physical objects, multimodal formats, and immersive environments.


Through a mix of lectures, hands-on exercises, and three creative projects, students will practice visual encoding, critique, narrative design, and ethical reasoning. They will create visualizations that are not only accurate and clear, but also inclusive, emotionally resonant, and human-centered. By the end of the course, students will be able to craft data stories across multiple modalities, communicate insights effectively, and thoughtfully evaluate the impact their visualizations have on diverse audiences.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this course, students will:

  1. Design clear, effective visualizations grounded in visual encoding and perception.
  2. Craft meaningful data stories through narrative structure, framing, and annotation.
  3. Create accessible and inclusive data experiences, including physical, sensory, and spatial forms.
  4. Build visualizations with multiple tools and select methods suited to different audiences and goals.
  5. Evaluate and communicate visualization work with attention to ethics, audience, and design impact.
